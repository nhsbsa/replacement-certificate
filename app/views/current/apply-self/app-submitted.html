What happens next

  • We'll check your entitlement using the details you've given us in your application.
  • We'll run a check to confirm your residency in the UK. This is not an identity check or a credit check, and it will not affect your credit score.
  • If your application is successful, we'll email your PRC to the treatment facilities so they can calculate any costs that can be covered. We'll also email you a copy.
  • If we need more information to help with your application, we may email you to ask for evidence.
  • It should only take a few minutes for this email to arrive, but it may take up to 24 hours. . You may need to check your spam or junk folder.

What a PRC covers

If you are eligible for a PRC, it provides the same cover as what would normally be covered by an existing European Health Insurance Card (EHIC), UK Global Health Insurance Card (UK GHIC), or UK European Health Insurance Card (UK EHIC).

This means that you'd be covered for emergency treatment costs as if you were a resident of that country.

What it does not cover

Most treatment costs are covered by a PRC, but there are some exceptions.

In some countries, a resident of that country may have to pay towards the cost of their treatment. This is known as a co-payment or a patient contribution. This means that you may need to pay for services that you would get for free on the NHS.

These payments are typically for services like GP or dentist consultations, prescriptions, or hospital stays.

Depending on the country you're claiming treatment, you may need to pay for services that you would get for free on the NHS. You may still receive an invoice for a co-payment if youve not paid one.

For more information on travelling in the European Economic Area (EEA) and Switzerland, including what a PRC covers in the country you're visiting, check the guidance on the GOV.UK website.

Important: Travel insurance

A PRC is not a substitute for travel insurance. It may not cover all health costs and never covers repatriation costs.
